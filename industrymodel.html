Condensed World Model

A single representation of what exists (entities), what is true right now (state), what is allowed (constraints & policies), what is remembered (memory), what can be tested safely (simulation), and what can be executed (tools & process flow). In real production environments, credibility requires audit-grade evidence attached to every quality-critical decision.

What makes this operational

  • Every stage writes explicit state (not just a narrative).
  • Every stage names the sensors/scanners that produce truth.
  • Decisions are bounded, deterministic, and governance-controlled.
  • Evidence references are first-class (traceable by Door ID).

What we do not claim here

  • No learning, no “smart guesses”, no heuristic autonomy claims.
  • No vendor lock-in: tools are described vendor-neutral.
  • No hidden recovery: resilience must be explicit and traceable.

Doorline (TVL) — Process Stages 1–6

The stages below describe a realistic BIW flow for a Front Left Door. Each stage is expressed in “world-model form”: entities, explicit sensors/scanners, deterministic state updates, bounded decisions, and audit evidence.

Starting point (Input)

Stamped/formed parts arrive from press shop / kitting: Inner Panel, Outer Panel, reinforcements (side impact beam, hinge/lock reinforcements, beltline/frame areas), plus optional inserts (studs, nuts, brackets, guides). Target: a geometrically stable, joined BIW door ready for downstream (paint, assembly).

Door ID created (TVL) Variant/sequence verified Kitting completeness evidence Trace opened (timestamped)
Process Stage 1 Pre-Assembly Inner Structure

Inner build-up (start the base body)

The door “begins” as an inner structure: the inner panel is positioned in fixtures and first reinforcements are placed and tack-fixed.

Entities
  • Inner Panel, fixture & clamps, base reinforcements, Door ID (TVL)
Signals (explicit sensors / scanners)
Barcode/QR or RFID reader (Door ID + variant) Industrial machine vision (presence/orientation) Clamp position / pressure sensors
State update
  • door.variant = confirmed
  • fixture.clamped = true
  • parts.present = inner + base reinforcements
Decision & governance
  • Edge: deterministic checks (presence + orientation + clamp OK)
  • Orchestrator: schedules transfer only if constraints pass
  • Guardian: blocks continuation on variant mismatch or clamp failure
Evidence
  • Vision snapshot ID + clamp telemetry appended to Door trace
  • Decision record (OK/HOLD) with rule reference
Process Stage 2 Reinforcements Functional Zones

Hinge, lock & crash structure

Critical functional zones are reinforced (hinge, lock, side impact). Presence and coarse positioning are verified before transfer.

Entities
  • Hinge/lock reinforcements, crash beam, studs/nuts/brackets, joining points
Signals (explicit sensors / scanners)
Inline structured light scanner (sub-mm alignment) Vision presence check (reinforcement completeness) Force/torque sensors (joining validation)
State update
  • reinforcements.hinge/reinforcements.lock/reinforcements.crash = present
  • join.tack_complete = true
Decision & governance
  • Edge: flags missing reinforcement → HOLD or rework route
  • Orchestrator: maintains sequence correctness + station readiness
  • Guardian: enforces “no transfer without presence evidence”
Evidence
  • 3D alignment summary (PASS/FAIL + deltas) stored
  • Joining force/torque trace attached to Door ID
Process Stage 3 Outer Panel Bonding & Hemming

Outer panel joining (surface risk begins)

The outer panel is applied and hemmed. This stage heavily influences surface appearance and later gap/flush behavior.

Entities
  • Outer panel, adhesive/sealer, hemming tooling (pre-hem / final hem)
Signals (explicit sensors / scanners)
Industrial vision / line scan (surface defects) Laser line scanner (edge/hem geometry) Adhesive bead monitoring (dispense quality)
State update
  • outer_panel.aligned = true/false
  • hemming.pre and hemming.final results stored
Decision & governance
  • Edge: detects surface risk (dent/scratch/imprint) → HOLD/REWORK
  • Orchestrator: validates sequence gates and station readiness
  • Guardian: enforces “no final hem without alignment evidence”
Evidence
  • Surface map snapshot + defect flags (if any)
  • Adhesive + hemming parameters appended to trace
Process Stage 4 Structural Completion Finalize Joining

Finalize structure & completeness

Remaining joining operations are completed and verified. Objective: structural completeness and stability.

Entities
  • Final weld/rivet/clinch points, hard-to-reach joins, BIW features
Signals (explicit sensors / scanners)
Weld monitoring (current/voltage/time signatures) Clinch/rivet verification sensors Optical measurement cell (spot verification)
State update
  • join.complete = true/false
  • integrity.score updated (rule-based)
Decision & governance
  • Edge: flags missing join evidence → HOLD
  • Orchestrator: routes to rework if policy allows
  • Guardian: prevents “OK” if any mandatory join lacks evidence
Evidence
  • Join parameter logs + completeness report linked to Door ID
Process Stage 5 Hard Quality Gate Geometry + Surface

Quality Gate: geometry & surface

The door is released or diverted. This is the audit-grade gate: gap/flush and surface dominate downstream cost, rework, and customer perception.

Entities
  • Measurement plan, tolerance rules, datum points, audit case, evidence bundle
Signals (explicit sensors / scanners)
Inline 3D scanner (structured light / laser) Surface inspection vision (defect classification) Gap/flush measurement system
State update
  • quality.geometry = PASS/FAIL + deltas
  • quality.surface = PASS/FAIL + defect tags
  • classification = OK / REWORK / SCRAP / HOLD
Decision & governance
  • Edge: deterministic pre-checks (no “intuition”)
  • Orchestrator: applies ruleset versioning and creates an audit case
  • Guardian: enforces release logic + evidence completeness
Evidence
  • Scan artifacts (IDs + summaries) attached to Door ID
  • Rule references + classification stored as an audit trace entry
Process Stage 6 Final Audit Handover

Final audit & handover

The inspected door is formally handed over — physically and digitally — with a final state and a complete evidence chain.

Entities
  • Door ID, batch/sequence, logistics buffer, downstream consumer, evidence links
Signals (explicit sensors / scanners)
Label print & scan verification RFID/QR tracking scan System status handshake
State update
  • door.state = RELEASED / QUARANTINE / HOLD
  • handover.complete = true
Decision & governance
  • Orchestrator: issues handover only if Stage 5 evidence exists
  • Guardian: blocks shipment on missing audit references
Evidence
  • Final trace entry links all prior stage evidence + final disposition
Audit-first interpretation

This is not “documentation for humans only”. It is intentionally structured so it can be executed and audited: each stage updates state, produces evidence, and remains governance-controlled.

Sensors & scanners (explicit)

This is the perception substrate of the world model — the source of truth for audits. Tool names are vendor-neutral but specific enough for technical credibility.

Identification

Prevents early mismatch errors by verifying variant, sequence, and Door ID.

Barcode / QR scanners RFID readers

Pose & presence

Ensures parts are present and correctly seated before joining operations proceed.

Industrial machine vision (presence/orientation) Position sensors Clamping pressure sensors

Joining verification

Captures process parameters as evidence for integrity and completeness.

Weld monitoring (I/V/t) Clinch / rivet confirmation sensors Force / torque transducers

Geometry verification

Measures deltas to nominal and feeds deterministic classification at the quality gate.

Inline structured light scanners (sub-mm) Laser line scanners (dimensional checks) Optical measurement cells / CMM (as needed)

Surface inspection

Detects dents, scratches, and imprints — the key risk during outer panel joining & hemming.

Industrial vision systems (high-res) Line scan cameras (surface mapping) Laser profiling (edge/hem quality)

Traceability & handover

Guarantees every quality-critical decision remains traceable and linked to the Door ID.

Label print/verify scan RFID/QR tracking scan System handshake (digital receipt)

From perception to action

The model follows a deliberate sequence — the factory thinks first, then acts.

  1. Perception — status and signals are captured.
  2. World Model Update — states are updated consistently.
  3. Reasoning & Simulation — possible decisions are evaluated.
  4. Governance Check — rules and approvals are enforced.
  5. Execution — only then actions are performed.
  6. Resilience — detect deviations, stabilize, and recover safely (with traceable outcomes).
